Patagonia Trail Explorers

Website Plan & Design Document

Site Name

Patagonia Trail Explorers

This name was selected because it clearly communicates the site's focus on hiking and exploration in the Patagonia region. "Trail Explorers" suggests adventure, discovery, and detailed trail knowledge, while "Patagonia" immediately identifies the geographic focus. The name appeals to both novice hikers seeking guidance and experienced adventurers looking for comprehensive trail information in this iconic wilderness destination.

Optional domain availability: patagonia-trails.org or trailexplorers-patagonia.com

Site Purpose

Patagonia Trail Explorers serves as a comprehensive digital hub for hiking enthusiasts planning adventures in the Patagonian wilderness of Argentina and Chile. The website provides detailed trail information including difficulty ratings, elevation profiles, seasonal accessibility, and current trail conditions. Users can access interactive planning tools, weather integration, customized packing lists, and GPS coordinates to ensure safe and well-prepared hiking experiences.

The site addresses the critical need for centralized, reliable trail information in a region where hikers currently must piece together data from multiple, often outdated sources. By providing accurate, up-to-date information in one location, the website promotes safer outdoor experiences while helping users discover the perfect trails for their skill level and interests.

User Scenarios

What are the best beginner-friendly trails in Torres del Paine for a 3-day hiking trip?

Sarah, a novice hiker from the United States, is planning her first multi-day hiking adventure in Patagonia. She needs trails that match her fitness level, detailed information about camping options, and guidance on what gear to bring for the unpredictable Patagonian weather.

Which trails are currently accessible in late autumn, and what are the current weather conditions?

Carlos, an experienced hiker from Buenos Aires, wants to plan a challenging trek during the shoulder season. He needs real-time trail condition updates, weather forecasts, and information about seasonal closures to avoid wasted travel and ensure safety.

How do I get GPS coordinates and offline maps for the Fitz Roy circuit trail?

Emma, a solo backpacker, is planning to hike the challenging Fitz Roy circuit. She needs precise navigation information, downloadable offline maps, and emergency contact information for remote areas where cell service is unavailable.

Color Schema

Patagonian Navy (#1a365d)

Usage: Primary headings, navigation bar, call-to-action buttons, and main branding elements. This deep navy blue evokes the dramatic Patagonian skies and provides excellent contrast (7.8:1 ratio) against white backgrounds for optimal readability.

Glacier Blue (#2c5282)

Usage: Secondary headings, accent elements, links, and interactive components. This refined blue represents the pristine glacial waters of Patagonia and offers strong contrast (5.9:1 ratio) while maintaining visual harmony.

Mountain Charcoal (#1a202c)

Usage: Body text, subheadings, and content areas. This deep charcoal provides excellent readability (12.6:1 contrast ratio) while evoking the rugged mountain landscapes of the region.

Alpine White (#ffffff)

Usage: Main background, card backgrounds, and light content areas. Pure white ensures maximum contrast ratios with all text colors while maintaining the clean, natural aesthetic.

Typography

Montserrat

Usage: Headings (H1, H2, H3), navigation elements, and prominent text. This modern, geometric sans-serif font provides excellent readability at large sizes and conveys the adventurous, professional nature of the site. Its clean lines work well for outdoor/adventure branding.

Explore Patagonia's Greatest Trails

Open Sans

Usage: Body text, descriptions, trail information, and all readable content. This highly legible humanist sans-serif ensures comfortable reading across all devices and screen sizes. It pairs excellently with Montserrat while maintaining accessibility standards.
